Taiwanese won't need visas to visit during Aichi expo Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi told the Taiwanese representative at the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation summit over the weekend that Japan will waive visas for tourists from Taiwan during the 2005 World Exposition in Aichi, the Central News Agency reported Monday.
The Taiwanese representative, Lee Yuan-tseh, who won the 1986 Nobel Prize in chemistry, said he had conversations on the matter with Koizumi on the sidelines of the Santiago summit, the Taiwanese news agency reported.
