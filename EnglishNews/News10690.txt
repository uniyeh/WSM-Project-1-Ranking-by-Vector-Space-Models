Higher oil prices need not doom a nation to inflation UBUD, Indonesia -- With high and volatile oil prices, it appears that a rough road is ahead for those countries with currencies that have become weaker relative to the U.S. dollar. Perhaps one of the biggest concerns is that Taiwan, as an importer of oil, may face a new wave of inflationary pressures that could exert upward pressures on interest rates and cause a slowdown in the rate of economic growth.
