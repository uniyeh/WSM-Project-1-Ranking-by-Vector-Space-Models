West Coast appreciates destiny with Asia LOS ANGELES — Serious intellectual narrowing can happen to even the brightest folk once nested down on the U.S. East Coast. They become preoccupied (almost neurotically, almost provincially) with the problems of the past — especially with the Middle East and Europe — and lose sight of the new problems and opportunities of this 21st century. They become lost in the inner space of a 20th-century time warp.
